Network Infrastructure in Wellington Central

By Sophia Meldrum

Wellington city network infrastructure is easily disregarded and unnoticeable until you search for it and soon realise it can be seen on every corner and every street you turn on to.

Cables

Cables are insulated wires that transmit electricity and telecommunication signals, in a protective casing. For companies such as chorus to install broadband they require cables that connect from powerlines to buildings. These cables provide us with a connection to a broader network.

CCTV

Closed-circuit television or CCTV is a form of video surveillance. These cameras allow you to watch the footage live and also record the footage for later reference. They are used most commonly as security devices and transmit the information they gather to broader networks which we can then access on other monitors. They are becoming increasingly popular.

Routers

Routers are devices for networking that forward packets of data between various computer networks. They are the traffic directing functions for the internet. Through routers, web pages, emails and various data is sent through the internet in the form of data packets.

Powerlines

Powerlines are fundamental in facilitating every network that we have. They are structures used to transmit and distribute electrical energy over large distances. They consist of uninsulated electrical cables and are usually suspended by poles and towers. They connect everything and everyone as they also provide phone lines and connection to the internet.
